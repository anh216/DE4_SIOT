Overview

A bright and sunny morning calls for similarly cheery music, right? But something a little more calming for those dark-skied, gloomy days? That is the essence of Here Comes The Sun!

We believe that the weather outside and the music in your ears have an interesting link. Take a look at some of our evidence, or give it a go yourself to test how accurate it really is!

Data Analysis

"There is a clear connection between what's in the skies, and what's on user's play queues," claims one of Spotify's researchers. We decided to put this statement to the test.

Data was collected in London around Christmas-time 2019, using OpenWeatherMap and Spotify Developer's API. Click the figures below to take a look some of our analysis.

Temperature and Humidity Patterns

These two plots show how the temperature (in degrees Celcius) and the humidity (percentage) varied across the week analysed. As you can see, they both have a periodic pattern during the day, but also an opposing relationship with each other.

Music Energy Patterns

This plot shows how the energy of the music tracks played varied across the week analysed. The energy of an audio track is measured from 0 to 1, and represents a perceptual measure of intensity and activity. Typically, energetic tracks feel fast, loud and noisy. There seems to be an obvious daily pattern!
